Features

Everything you need for perfect address data.

Autocomplete Suggestions

As users type, Google Places suggests complete, validated addresses. Selecting a suggestion populates all address fields automaticallyâ€”street, city, state, postal code, and country.

Autocomplete works for addresses worldwide, supporting local formats and conventions. Users spend seconds instead of minutes on address entry.

Address Validation

Every suggested address is validated against Google's authoritative database. Invalid or undeliverable addresses are flagged before they enter your CRM.

Validation catches common errors like non-existent street numbers, incorrect postal codes, and misspelled city names. Data quality improves from the point of entry.

Standardized Formatting

Addresses are automatically formatted according to local postal standards. "123 main st apt 4b" becomes "123 Main St, Apt 4B" with proper capitalization and abbreviations.

Consistent formatting improves searchability, reporting, and professional communication. Mail merge documents look polished and accurate.

Geocoding

Every validated address includes latitude and longitude coordinates. Use geocodes for mapping, territory assignment, distance calculations, and location-based reporting.

Geocoding enables powerful location analyticsâ€”visualize customer distribution, optimize travel routes, and identify geographic opportunities.

Country-Specific Formats

Address formats vary by country. The toolkit adapts to local conventionsâ€”postal code placement, state/province requirements, and address line structures.

International businesses benefit from accurate formatting across all markets. No more forcing global addresses into US-centric templates.

Batch Cleanup

Existing CRM data can be cleaned in batches. Upload a list of records or select existing contacts to validate and standardize addresses using Google Places.

Batch cleanup identifies problematic addresses for manual review. Improve historical data quality without re-entering everything manually.
